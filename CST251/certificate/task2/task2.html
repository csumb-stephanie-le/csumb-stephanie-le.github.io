Cameron Boyce

1999-2019

About

Cameron Boyce was an American actor, dancer, singer, and humanitarian. Boyce was born on May 28, 1999 in Los Angeles, California. An entertainer since childhood, he discovered his love for performing when he learned to dance. Boyce was trained in jazz, tap, ballet, modern, hip-hop, and break dancing. He was recognized worldwide for his Disney roles in the TV show Jessie and Descendents film franchise, among others. Cameron passed away on July 6, 2019, in his sleep after suffering a seizure. His death was linked to epilepsy.


Timeline of Cameron Boyce's Life:

  • May 1999 - Born in Los Angeles, California
  • 2006 - Began his modeling career at the age of 7, appearing in the Disney Store catalog, and has since been featured in print advertisements for Garnett Hill, Wilsons Leather, Jaxx Pacific, Nestle and K-Mart, as well as appeared in more than 20 commercials
  • May 2008 - Made his debut as a child actor in the music video "That Green Gentlemen (Things Have Changed)," appearing as a younger version of Panic! at the Disco guitarist Ryan Ross
  • July 2008 - Made his television debut in the series General Hospital: Night Shift as a recurring character named Michael
  • August 2008 - Made his feature film debut with a starring role in the horror movie Mirrors, as well as appearing in the mystery-thriller film Eagle Eye
  • June 2010 - Starred as Keith, son of Adam Sandler's character, in the comedy film Grown Ups and reprised his role of Keith Feder in the sequel Grown Ups 2 which was released in 2013 and also appeared in the web series The Legend of Extraordinary Dancers
  • April 2011 - Made a guest appearance on Disney Channel's comedy series Good Luck Charlie
  • September 2011 - Casted in the starring role of Luke Ross in the Disney comedy series Jessie
  • 2012 - Starred in the titular role of Jake in the second and third seasons of the Disney Junior series Jake and the Never Land Pirates and was part of an ensemble cast that won a Young Artist Award for best performance in the film “Judy Moody and the Not Bummer Summer”
  • July 2015 - Starred as one of the main cast members in Disney Channel's Descendents, playing Carlos, son of Cruella de Vil and later reprised the character for the animated shorts Descendants: Wicked World and in the sequels Descendants 2 and Descendants 3 as well as starring as Conor in Disney XD's Gamer's Guide to Pretty Much Everything

  • 2017 - Won a Daytime Emmy Award with Disney XD for "Outstanding Promotional Announcement" for participating in the series “Timeless Heroes—Be Inspired," honoring Black History Month
  • 2018 - Launched a campaign on his birthday that raised more than $27,000 to build two freshwater wells in Swaziland
  • April 2018 - Received the Pioneering Spirit Award at an annual gala for the Thirst Project, a non-profit organization that raises awareness of the global water crisis and provides freshwater wells in 3rd world countries and after Cameron's passing, this award was named the “Cameron Boyce Pioneering Spirit Award” in honor of him
  • July 2018 - Announced that Boyce was playing the lead in the indie thriller film Runt which was released in 2020
  • April 2019 - Performed in the music video for "Almost (Sweet Music)" by Hozier and choreographed the dance with Christine Flores, who appeared in the video alongside him
  • July 2019 - Died in Los Angeles, California at the age of 20 from a seizure caused by SUDEP (Sudden Unexpected Death in Epilespy)
